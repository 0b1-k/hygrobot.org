What is HyGroBot?

A modular control system for your garden

HyGroBot is an open-source electronic gardening assistant.

HyGroBot is built with small electronic modules, so that it can grow with your requirements and budget. The brain of the system is an extended Raspberry Pi embedded computer. This central module can control sensors, cameras, pumps, valves, etc., in order to automate the tasks associated with growing and maintaining your plants in good health.

HyGroBot can water your plants, turn lights on and off, oxygenate water, distribute nutrients, and measure temperature, pH, humidity and water levels. It can also decide when to do all these things, and requires as little intervention from you as possible. It will however keep you informed through its notification system.

A friendly user interface

Because the brain of the system is a full Linux computer, it can not only control the growth of your plants, but also give you full visibility on their health through its embedded web server. Connect HyGroBot to your local network, and you'll be able to point your phone, tablet, or computer to it.

The HyGroBot application will give you the current status of your plants, as well as simple controls if you need to make a change. Eventually, you'll be able to program HyGroBot through a simple system of events and actions.

A growing community

We believe that sharing experiences in a community is the best path to success. A big goal for HyGroBot is to build a community of amateur and professional gardeners who share a common passion and philosophy, and who are ready to give as much as they receive. Whether you found the perfect settings for a particular strain, or just improved on somebody else's, we would like nothing more than for you to be able to share this back.

In order to do so, we will soon provide the tools and infrastructure that will enable you to share recipes and experiences.

What it's not

HyGroBot is designed as a control system, not as a full end-to-end plantation-in-a-box. You can use HyGroBot to augment your existing system, or you can build a new system around it. To do so, you will have to build a structure, install HyGroBot in it, as well as find fans, lights, and containers. This site will eventually help you find local suppliers for all of these things, but we are not going to sell and ship them to you.

What we are definitely going to provide is know-how, experience, the HyGroBot brains and modules as open source hardware and software, and a way to share your experiences with others.
